ELEPHANTS

Elephants are the largest living land animals. Three living species are currently recognised: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant. They are the only surviving members of the family Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea; extinct relatives include mammoths and mastodons.

Elephants are known for their extraordinary memory, remembering locations of water sources and food over long distances, even after many years, as noted by Trafalgar Tours

Female elephants have the longest pregnancy of any mammal, lasting a remarkable 22 months, as reported by Action Change.

Elephant trunks are not just used for smelling but also for picking up objects, breathing, drinking water, and even cooling down with dust baths
